# Usage Notes

This reference file supports `research-source-scan`.

## When this skill is most useful

- A thesis-first decision memo rather than a generic summary.
- A short source set that favors recent, primary, and attributable references.
- A concrete recommendation that says adopt now, trial first, or defer.
- An explicit statement of uncertainty when evidence is weak or mixed.
- Next actions that are concrete enough to test rather than generic placeholders.

## Delivery hints

- Thesis matters: the reader should finish with one memorable conclusion, not a pile of notes.
- Evidence drives quality: unsupported claims reduce trust faster than brief but well-sourced analysis.
- A short decision brief is stronger than a long generic summary when the reader needs to choose what to do next.
- For a small team, the memo should explicitly distinguish adopt-now conditions from defer conditions.
- Named sources beat anonymous source categories because they can be checked and challenged.
- The strongest next actions describe what to test first, how long it should take, and what success looks like.
- Independent benchmarks or industry syntheses can keep the memo from feeling like vendor collateral when the source pool is crowded with product docs.
