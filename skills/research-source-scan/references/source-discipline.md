# Source Discipline

## Source ladder

- Prefer official docs, standards, changelogs, engineering writeups, and attributable operator notes.
- Use community posts mainly to surface issues that you then verify elsewhere.
- Name the source directly instead of citing anonymous buckets like industry research or developer consensus.

## Source bullet pattern

- Use the format: Source name (date or freshness marker): fact. Why it matters: decision implication.
- Keep source bullets short, but include enough detail that the reader could plausibly re-find the source.
- If freshness matters, include the year or month-year instead of leaving time implicit.
- If you cannot name a real source, lower confidence or omit the point instead of filling space with community consensus.

## Evidence quality checks

- Avoid repeating the same idea in the source section and the evidence section without adding a new implication.
- When a claim depends on scale, say who it applies to: small team, regulated team, latency-sensitive product, or multi-provider stack.
- If a source is weak, say so directly and lower confidence rather than smoothing it over.
